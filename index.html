SECTION 01

The lens.

One question for every partner: does it help us build durable infrastructure, or pull us toward bespoke, services-led work?

Various types of channel partners hand us distribution, but distribution is not the business. We're building the lasting infrastructure layer with our Intelligence Engine; partners accelerate that, they don't redefine it.

SECTION 03

Partner types in detail.

Type 01

Data partners.

TOP FIT TODAY · ESRI (GEOSPATIAL)

Purpose. Enrich the graph with 3rd-party datasets historically too expensive for mid-market to license or implement, embedded once into the Intelligence Engine and surfaced across every client deployment. Spatial is the wedge, a physical-world layer; financial, identity, and regulatory data extend the same pattern into the digital dimension.

Esri / ArcGIS, in flight: ingest publicly available story maps and public spatial layers, encode with an ontology, and stand up a spatially-aware graph an AI can reason over (~1–2 months to build and validate). Logistics is the anchor use case (Ozinga, Echo, Dorian); insurance is the first replication test. The multi-tool unlock is fusing outputs from several ArcGIS tools and letting the graph make the decision. Ingesting story maps up front keeps the ontology in Found's graph, not in live ArcGIS calls, which mitigates dependency risk. Still informal: next step is concrete case studies and workflow examples from Jay.

Value we provide them

  • Mid-market distribution without their own enterprise sales motion
  • Recurring, sticky consumption embedded in a platform, not a one-off integration
  • Their data made actionable via the graph and Insight Patterns

Value they provide us

  • Differentiated enrichment no competitor can cheaply replicate
  • A deeper, defensible graph: relationships across their data + client data
  • Enrichment that benefits every client and compounds the moat

Best fits

  • Spatial / geospatial (anchor): Esri / ArcGIS, story maps + public layers; Mapbox, Maxar, HERE
  • Logistics / distribution: traffic & telematics (HERE, INRIX), freight rates & capacity (DAT, FreightWaves SONAR), RFID / sensor fusion
  • Insurance, underwriting & risk: Verisk, CoreLogic, Moody's RMS, LexisNexis Risk
  • Financial services: firmographics & entity (D&B, S&P Global), transaction / vendor-spend (Plaid), market & pricing (ICE, Bloomberg)
  • Cross-industry: identity / entity resolution (LexisNexis, Moody's Orbis)

Commitment & payment options

Embedded license
Found licenses the dataset; pass-through baked into platform pricing.
Rev-share on enrichment
Partner earns a share when their data layer is consumed by clients.
Reciprocal / co-marketing
Data-for-exposure where joint logos and case studies carry the value.
Type 02

Technology partners.

TOP FITS TODAY · SURREALDB, DATABRICKS

Purpose. The infrastructure the Intelligence Engine is built on or integrates with, the database, retrieval, and orchestration components that power the substrate. The aim is to co-engineer durable capability and embed deeper into the stack, not ship brittle one-off integrations.

Value we provide them

  • A flagship production reference in a demanding enterprise context
  • Roadmap feedback from a real AI-memory / graph workload
  • Joint technical content and AI-infrastructure credibility

Value they provide us

  • Core infrastructure we don't have to build, a faster, more capable platform
  • Co-engineering and priority support on the substrate we depend on
  • Preferential terms and early access to capabilities

Best fits

  • Graph / multi-model databases: SurrealDB, Neo4j, TigerGraph
  • Data & analytics / lakehouse: Databricks, Snowflake
  • Embedding, retrieval & vector: Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant
  • Model providers & inference: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Vertex
  • Connector / ingestion & orchestration: Fivetran, Airbyte, dbt

Commitment & payment options

Design / co-eng partner
Discounted or credited usage in exchange for reference + roadmap input.
Standard commercial
Negotiated enterprise terms with joint-marketing commitments attached.
Mutual referral
Each side refers fitting deals; no money changes hands, logos do.
Type 03

Consulting / SIs.

TOP FIT TODAY · ABERDEEN ADVISORS (CONSULTING)

Purpose. SIs and consulting firms that build top-of-funnel and deliver the last mile under their own client relationships, running "powered by Found" beneath their MSA. They own the relationship and delivery; Found owns the platform and the compounding intelligence.

Value we provide them

  • An infrastructure layer that makes their engagements stickier and higher-margin
  • "Powered by Found" capability they can't build themselves
  • Recurring platform revenue beyond one-time project fees

Value they provide us

  • Warm, qualified top-of-funnel into mid-market enterprises
  • Trusted relationships and contract vehicles (their MSA)
  • Delivery capacity and the last-mile services we don't want to own

Best fits & in-flight

  • Aberdeen Advisors, ~200-person, ex-Deloitte; channel + investor; $250M–$5B mid-market
  • Joint engagements: Novant Health (CapEx auto-coding), Hanger (disallowments recovery)
  • Boutique & vertical consultancies in regulated industries: West Monroe, Slalom, Crowe, Baker Tilly
  • Referral-only sources (e.g. Ozinga's CIO, finder's fee interest)

Commitment & payment options

Powered-by / reseller
Found delivered under partner MSA; partner marks up, Found keeps platform fee.
Referral / finder's fee
One-time or outcome-credit rebate on referred, closed deals.
Co-sell revenue share
Shared pipeline with agreed split on jointly-sourced ACV.
Type 04

Private equity partners.

TOP FITS TODAY · SUMMIT, TRIVEST, SVOBODA CAPITAL

Purpose. PE and growth funds that open their portfolios, turning one fund relationship into warm, board-sponsored intros across many portfolio companies. Found becomes a value-creation lever the fund deploys portfolio-wide.

Value we provide them

  • A portfolio-wide value-creation lever: margin, efficiency, enterprise value
  • One platform deployable across portcos, not a per-company build
  • A differentiated edge for operating teams and diligence

Value they provide us

  • Warm, sponsored intros to multiple portcos from one relationship
  • Board-level credibility that shortens the sales cycle
  • A repeatable, high-leverage motion: one fund, many enterprises

Best fits & ICP caution

  • Top fits: Summit, Trivest, Svoboda Capital
  • Other lower / mid-market funds whose portcos fit our ICP: Shore Capital, Mainsail, Frontenac, Alpine Investors
  • ICP flag: revenue ranges differ by fund, e.g. Summit's core portcos can run $400M+, Trivest and Svoboda skew smaller, screen each fund's range against our band
  • Screen each fund's portfolio against ICP before committing, score ICP alignment per fund

Commitment & payment options

Per-intro / finder's fee
Fee or outcome credit on each closed portfolio-company deal.
Portfolio platform agreement
Master terms with the fund; portco-level orders underneath.
Value-creation partnership
Success-based, tied to portfolio operating outcomes.
Type 05

Embedded / OEM (ISV) partners.

"INTELLIGENCE ENGINE INSIDE" · WORKFLOW-SPECIFIC POINT SOLUTIONS

Purpose. Workflow-specific point solutions, the AI-native apps that own a single high-value workflow in a vertical, embed Found as the memory / intelligence substrate beneath their product, the "Intelligence Engine inside." Found is the thing being consumed, infrastructure others build on, not a service we deliver. This is the inverse of Technology partners: there Found consumes the partner's infrastructure; here the partner consumes Found.

Value we provide them

  • A memory / graph / retrieval substrate they don't have to build, under their own UI
  • A fast path to AI-native features without standing up their own data layer
  • A differentiated, defensible product their competitors can't cheaply match

Value they provide us

  • Distribution into their installed base, every embedded app is a new consumption channel
  • Compounding structured data: each deployment feeds more signal into the graph
  • The purest expression of the thesis, Found as infrastructure that scales without services drag

Best fits

  • Legal: Harvey, AI workflow for contracts, research, and matter work
  • Logistics: Augment, AI agent for freight and supply-chain workflows
  • Insurance / financial services: V7 Go, document-to-decision AI for underwriting and diligence
  • Selection bar: owns a high-value workflow, AI-native roadmap, and data that deepens the graph

Commitment & payment options

Embedded OEM license
Usage / seat royalty on Found consumed under the partner's product.
Platform fee + rev-share
Base platform fee plus a share of the AI-feature revenue they unlock.
Co-build / design partner
Discounted embed for reference, roadmap input, and graph data rights.
SECTION 04

Scoring partner fit.

Rate every prospective partner 1–5 on six criteria, weight by type, place in a tier. The point is to enforce two things on every deal: mutual value and alignment to the infrastructure thesis.

01

Strategic alignment

Reinforces Found as infrastructure, or drifts us toward services and diagnostics?

HIGHEST WEIGHT
02

Mutual value

Clear, durable value both directions, or one side carrying the relationship?

HIGHEST WEIGHT
03

ICP overlap

Reaches our target: mid-market, data-rich, regulated verticals (insurance, construction, banking, healthcare, CRE).

04

Graph contribution

Deepens the compounding graph: new data, new capability, or new structured signal.

05

Effort vs. return

Lift to launch and maintain proportional to the pipeline, capability, or moat created.

06

Durability & defensibility

Makes the platform stickier, or creates a dependency that erodes the moat?

Weighting by type

How much each criterion counts toward a given type's score. The same 1–5 ratings are multiplied by these weights, so a Highest criterion moves the score three times as much as a Low one. This is what makes the framework behave differently per type without changing the criteria.

The override

The infrastructure test trumps the score.

A partner can score high on funnel and still be wrong if the deal makes us the diagnostic firm. Anything that pushes Found toward bespoke services is capped at "Watch" until restructured so Found owns the platform and IP, and the partner owns the relationship and last-mile delivery. Distribution is welcome; becoming a consultancy is not.

SECTION 05

Scoring in practice, Aberdeen Advisors.

A worked example on the Consulting / SI weighting. Aberdeen is a ~200-person consulting firm (ex-Deloitte), consulting + investor, targeting $250M–$5B mid-market, with two joint engagements already identified (Novant Health, Hanger).

CriterionConsulting / SI weightScoreWhy
Strategic alignmentHighest2Consulting firms deliver diagnostics and leave; Found compounds and gets stickier over time. Structural mismatch.
Mutual valueHighest3We give them a lot; in return, logos plus access and pipeline at this stage, but thin durable value long term.
ICP overlapHighest5$250M–$5B mid-market, regulated verticals. Dead-center ICP.
Graph contributionLow3Indirect, but engagements (CapEx, disallowments) feed real data in.
Effort vs. returnHigh4Powered-by enablement needed, but pipeline return is high.
Durability / defensibilityMed3Partner-dependency risk, mitigated because Found owns the platform and IP.

Weight values: Highest 3 · High 2 · Med 1.5 · Low 1. Weighted average = sum(score × weight) ÷ sum(weight) = 45.5 ÷ 13.5.

Weighted result

Aberdeen Advisors → Build

3.4

Read: Build, not Anchor. Strong distribution and ICP fit, but the strategic-alignment gap and thin long-term mutual value pull it down, the tension to manage. The infrastructure test still applies: holds only while the deal stays "powered by Found" with Found owning the platform and IP; as pure services with Found as subcontractor, it caps at Watch.
